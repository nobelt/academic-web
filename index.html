Nobel Truong

Currently at the University of California, Los Angeles. Previously at the University of California, Berkeley.

Nobel Truong is a researcher in computer science at UCLA currently advised by Adnan Darwiche. Her interests lie in knowledge representation, knowledge compilation, causal structures, and causal discovery. More broadly, she is researching methods in formal and informal hardware and software verification and automated reasoning. Truong received her Bachelor's degree in philosophy from UC Berkeley where she was advised by John Campbell. Her undergraduate thesis was on employing causal structures as a formal framework for defining empathy.

Truong's slides on computer science and other related topics can be found here. In a second life, Truong also maintain a small industrial design practice.
